01 · Introduction

Why Build a
Spectrophotometer?

Conventional laboratory-grade spectrophotometers are essential tools for material analysis — but their high cost, large size, and non-portability restrict their use in educational labs, field applications, and industrial quality control.

"Every material possesses a unique reflectance spectrum — a chemical and physical fingerprint enabling accurate, non-destructive identification."

This project proposes a low-cost portable Reflectance Spectrophotometer using a common camera and diffraction grating to capture visible light spectra (400–700 nm). RGB pixel intensities are mapped mathematically to wavelengths, and a Deep Learning model classifies materials in real-time through a user-friendly GUI.

⚡

LED Intensity Uniformity

Consistent illumination with low-cost LEDs is difficult. Inconsistent brightness causes varying reflectance measurements impacting training data quality.

Solution: Arduino PWM control trials with different LED configurations until uniform illumination is achieved.
📐

Camera Angle Alignment

Exact angle between sample, diffraction element, and camera is critical. Wrong angle captures partial spectrum, preventing full 400–700nm mapping.

Solution: Iterative mechanical adjustment calibrated against known atomic spectral lines.
